What the job actually is

Strip the jargon — there are three jobs

1
Sit with the problem
Understand it before you reach for a solution.
Why: stops you from rebuilding a broken process in a shinier new tool.
2
Frame the hypothesis
Outcome, success, constraints — written down.
Why: turns "we need a better X" into something you can actually test.
3
Run learning loops
Build small, evaluate, decide, scale.
Why: replaces big-bang bets with small, recoverable moves.

None of that is "write a PRD" or "own a roadmap." Those are outputs. The three jobs are the actual work.

The one mental model

The Confidence Line

Every decision sits somewhere from "we're guessing" to "we know."

We're guessing Hypothesis + evidence We know

The whole job is moving the needle — cheaply and honestly.

Cheaply

Small tests — not three-month projects.

Honestly

Real evidence — not vibes or seniority.
